Population

Egpyt is the home of more than 100 million people, up by 20 million people since 2000. Of those 100 million people, more than 12 million people are squeezed into the capital, Cairo. It ranks 37th in the world for the densest city fitting in around 20,000 people per square kilometer. Since the city is populated with people, pollution, traffic, and narrow streets are daily struggles people face when commuting to school or their jobs. Luckily, the city has adopted trains, subways, taxis, "tok toks" and even microbuses for dropping and picking up hundreds if not thousands of people a day to get from one end of a long street to another one.

Poverty

Because of Egypt's rough history with past presidents and foreign affairs, many of the people of the population suffur from poverty. It was noted that the for every 3 people, 1 is living in poor conditions. Thus, around a whopping 33.3 millon people live in poverty today. This is up from 16.7% since 2000 and 27.8% since 2015. Despite the insane numbers, most people make ends meet at the end of the day by selling fruits or vegetables, as well as being doormans to the 15 story high apartment buildings protecting the building and responding to orders from apartment renters or buyers in exhchange for a free small room in the basement of the building.

Economy

Despite the unfortunate poverty rates, the Egyptian people see hope in the future to come. In the past, Egypt's currency has inflated compared to the USD. For reference, back in 2000 the Egyptian pound was around 6 pounds per 1 USD. On October 2016, the Egyptian pound went up to almost 9 pounds, and one month later in November, shot up to 18 pounds. Since 2018, the pound has been stable at the 17 mark, but it wasn't until recently in early 2019 when Egyptians saw the pound decreasing slowly. Today, the pound sits at almost 16 pounds per dollar, thus, showing hope for the future of Egypt.
